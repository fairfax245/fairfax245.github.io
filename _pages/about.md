I am **Patrick Fairfax**, a literary scholar and educator specializing in the evolution of narrative structures from the Gothic tradition to postmodernity. Having earned my Ph.D. from **Boston University**, my work explores how contemporary literature navigates shifting cultural and linguistic landscapes.

## Education
* **Ph.D. in English** | Boston University (2020 – 2024)
  * *Dissertation: Postmodern Narratives in Contemporary Literature*
* **M.A. in English** | University of Alabama (2017 – 2019)
  * *Thesis: The Evolution of Gothic Literature in the 20th Century*
* **B.A. in English** (Honors) | New York University (2015 – 2017)

## Research & Academic Experience
My academic journey has been defined by a deep dive into literary analysis and critical theory. 

* **Graduate Research Assistant** | University of Alabama (2017 – 2024)
  * Conducted extensive research in cultural studies and assisted faculty in editing high-level academic manuscripts and conference papers.
* **Teaching Assistant** | Boston University (2016 – 2020)
  * Led undergraduate discussion sections, focusing on improving student rhetoric and composition skills through detailed feedback and custom-designed learning materials.

## Technical Skills & Expertise
Beyond literary theory, I am proficient in the digital tools essential for modern humanities research:
* **Writing & Editing:** Academic manuscript preparation, peer-review standards, and Chicago/MLA style expertise.
* **Digital Tools:** LaTeX for professional typesetting, Zotero for bibliographic management, and MS Office Suite.
* **Methodologies:** Qualitative research, archival analysis, and linguistic criticism.

## Professional Affiliations
* Member, **Modern Language Association (MLA)**
